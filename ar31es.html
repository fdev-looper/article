What Is SIM and eSIM The Basics Everyone Should Know

Understanding the technology behind your phone number

The Basics of SIM and eSIM

SIM stands for Subscriber Identity Module that tiny chip inside your phone which connects you to your mobile network. It carries your unique number, your network ID, and acts like your digital passport every time you make a call, send a text, or receive an OTP.

But with newer phones, that physical chip is slowly disappearing replaced by something called eSIM, short for Embedded SIM. Unlike the old SIM card you could remove or swap, an eSIM is built directly into your phone’s motherboard. It’s activated digitally using a QR code or network profile provided by your carrier.

The convenience is great no more cutting SIMs or visiting stores. You can switch numbers or carriers online within minutes. But here’s the catch: not every number or device supports eSIM yet. Only phones that have eSIM hardware (like iPhones, Pixels, and newer Samsung models) can use it, and it still needs to be linked with your existing mobile number through your telecom provider.

In short your number isn’t created as a new eSIM automatically. It’s converted from a physical SIM to a digital one after identity verification. And that’s exactly where the danger hides if someone tricks your telecom into approving that conversion without your consent, they don’t just take your number… they take you.

Real-Life eSIM Scam Cases in India

Shocking incidents that highlight the scam's impact

Case 1: The Rs 4 Lakh Disappearance

According to warnings issued by India's Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C), one victim lost Rs 4 lakh from their bank account without sharing any OTP or ATM details. The scammer simply sent a fraudulent eSIM activation link. Within minutes, the victim’s phone went silent, and their money vanished into thin air.

Case 2: Noida Woman's Rs 27 Lakh Nightmare

As reported by The Economic Times, a 44-year-old woman from Noida fell victim to an eSIM fraud that cost her Rs 27 lakh. The attackers even took out a Rs 7.40 lakh loan in her name. The whole attack happened so fast that she didn’t realize her SIM was compromised until the damage was already done.

Case 3: Mumbai’s 4-Minute Bank Robbery

The Times of India reported a case where a Mumbai resident lost Rs 4 lakh in just minutes after clicking a malicious eSIM link. Despite having blocked ATM and UPI access, the fraudsters bypassed all security measures once they gained control of the phone number through eSIM swap.

These cases are shocking but they also show one thing clearly: this scam is fast, silent, and can hit anyone.

How This Scam Actually Works

The step-by-step process scammers use to steal your identity

Targeting the Victim

Scammers often send fake eSIM activation links via WhatsApp, SMS, or email. The message looks official maybe from your telecom operator and urges immediate action.

Tricking the Telecom Provider

Once the victim clicks the link and enters minimal details, scammers use social engineering or phishing to convince the telecom provider to activate a new eSIM on the attacker’s device.

Phone and Number Takeover

When the eSIM is activated on the attacker’s device, the victim’s phone suddenly loses signal. All calls, OTPs, and verification messages are now routed to the scammer.

Money and Identity Theft

With access to the number, attackers can reset banking apps, authorize transactions, and even take loans or investments in the victim’s name all without needing OTPs directly from the victim.

5 Practical Tips to Stay Safe and Smart

Verify any eSIM activation request never click unsolicited links. Contact your carrier directly.
Set up a telecom PIN / passcode adds an extra layer before anyone can port or convert your SIM.
Use hardware 2FA for banking and important apps app-based OTPs alone are not enough.
Monitor bank activity closely alert the bank immediately if anything looks suspicious.
Don’t share personal details like Aadhaar, PAN, or OTP over messages or calls.

Today's Dictionary

SIM (Subscriber Identity Module)

A small chip in your phone that connects you to your mobile network, carrying your unique number and network ID, acting as a digital passport for calls, texts, and OTPs.

eSIM (Embedded SIM)

A digital SIM embedded in a phone’s motherboard, activated via a QR code or network profile, allowing number or carrier switches without a physical card.

OTP (One-Time Password)

A temporary code sent to a user’s phone or email for secure authentication, often used in banking and online transactions.

Phishing

A fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity in digital communication, such as fake emails or links.

Social Engineering

Manipulative tactics used by scammers to trick individuals or organizations into revealing confidential information or performing specific actions.

2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)

A security process requiring two different verification methods to access an account, such as a password and a hardware token or OTP.

Aadhaar

A 12-digit unique identification number issued by the Indian government, used for identity verification across various services.

PAN (Permanent Account Number)

A 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Indian Income Tax Department, used for financial transactions and tax purposes.
