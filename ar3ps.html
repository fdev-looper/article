What is Phishing?

üìß Definition & Overview

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers contact targets by email, telephone, or text message while posing as legitimate institutions to steal sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking details, and passwords.

üì± Types of Phishing

Email Phishing: Most common form using fraudulent emails

SMiShing (SMS): Text message-based phishing attacks

Vishing (Voice): Phone-based social engineering

Social Media Phishing: Scams through social platforms

Red Flags & Warning Signs

üìß

Suspicious Sender Address

Check for misspelled domain names (e.g., @amaz0n.comShould be @amazon.com instead of @amazon.com) or public email domains being used for business communications.

üîó

Unexpected Links

Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. Beware of URLs that don't match the legitimate website's domain or use URL shorteners to hide destinations.

‚ö°

Urgent or Threatening Language

Phishing emails often create false urgency ("Your account will be suspended!") to prompt quick action without careful thinking.

üéÅ

Too Good to Be True

Offers that seem overly generous or promise unexpected rewards are often bait to get you to click malicious links or provide information.

üìù

Spelling and Grammar Errors

Professional organizations proofread their communications. Multiple errors often indicate a phishing attempt from non-native speakers.

üìé

Suspicious Attachments

Unexpected email attachments, especially with extensions like .exe, .scr, or .zip, may contain malware or ransomware.

Prevention Techniques

üîç

Verify the Sender

Always check the sender's email address carefully. When in doubt, contact the organization directly using official contact information from their website.

üñ±Ô∏è

Hover Before Clicking

Hover your cursor over links to see the actual destination URL. Only click if the link matches the expected legitimate destination.

üîê

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

2FA adds an extra security layer, making it much harder for attackers to access accounts even with compromised passwords.

üîÑ

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and security software to protect against known vulnerabilities.

üõ°Ô∏è

Use Anti-Phishing Tools

Enable anti-phishing features in email clients and web browsers. Consider using dedicated security software solutions.

üéì

Educate Yourself & Others

Stay informed about latest phishing techniques and share knowledge with friends, family, and colleagues regularly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

üö® Critical Mistakes People Make

Clicking First, Thinking Second: Always pause and analyze before clicking any links or downloading attachments

Trusting Display Names: Email display names can be easily faked - always check the actual sender address

Ignoring URL Mismatches: If an email claims to be from Amazon but links to a different domain, it's likely fake

Providing Information Under Pressure: Legitimate organizations never demand immediate action with threats

‚ùå Phishing vs Legitimate

Phishing: Uses urgency, threats, generic greetings, suspicious links, and poor grammar

Legitimate: Professional tone, personalized content, official domains, proper spelling, and no pressure tactics

üîÑ When in Doubt

Stop: Don't click anything immediately

Verify: Contact the organization directly through official channels

Report: Forward suspicious emails to your IT department or the impersonated organization
