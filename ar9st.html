The Subscription Trap

How Tech Companies Are Quietly Draining Your Bank Account Through Digital Subscriptions

💳 Financial Security
📅 September 2025
⏱️ 12 min read

From 2GB/day internet plans to unlimited data, OTT subscriptions, AI tools, and gaming services

How everyday digital products quietly trick users into paying more than they realize

I was sitting alone, watching a series on an OTT platform. In another room, my family members were engaged in their own activities. It was a casual holiday, and I paused for a moment. I thought, "Wait… is this who I was five years ago?" Back then, we used to sit together, watch films, laugh, and my father and I would watch cricket on television together. Everything has changed so much.

Reflecting further, I thought about life before all this digital chaos—before OTT, unlimited data, AI apps, or cloud storage. Generations before us, especially those born before 1998 (pre-Gen Z), had a simpler life. People spent more time together, outdoors, and in face-to-face interactions. Life wasn't dictated by notifications, subscriptions, or the fear of missing out.

I am writing this not just from an emotional perspective but also from a technical and cybersecurity lens. I used to indulge in all these digital services myself—every OTT subscription, every AI tool, every "free trial" I clicked on—but over time, I realized how easy it is to get trapped. Where once our expenses were limited to a simple DTH connection and a mobile recharge, now we're layered in Wi-Fi plans, OTT subscriptions, unlimited data packs, click-and-subscribe apps, AI tools, cloud storage, gaming platforms, and fintech apps.

Have you ever wondered how 2GB internet packs turned into unlimited plans? According to the Ormax OTT Audience Report 2025, India's OTT audience has reached 601.2 million users, representing 41.1% of the country's population. The majority are young adults, primarily between 15–34 years old, with a significant portion in the 22–30 age bracket.

Some questions naturally arise: Are we overdoing it? Do we really need all these subscriptions? Weren't we enjoying life before they existed? The question is not about generations—it's about whether human life is meant to revolve around technology and isolation. Humans are inherently social beings, not solitary like a koala or a tiger lying on a bed.

One of the biggest traps is the "Free Trial". You've probably noticed how they advertise it: "Link, access, cancel anytime." That one-click subscription is easy, but unsubscribing often becomes frustrating for a normal user. Many services make it intentionally cumbersome to cancel, quietly locking users into recurring payments.

Examples of Subscription Traps

OTT Platforms (Netflix, Disney+ Hotstar, Amazon Prime)

Monthly costs range from ₹149–₹999, depending on the plan and features.

These apps do not need access to your contacts or call logs, yet many users grant unnecessary permissions without thinking.

AI Tools (ChatGPT Plus, MidJourney, Canva Pro)

Monthly subscriptions from ₹500–₹1500, encouraging continuous renewal for "extra features."

Free trials lure users into long-term payments with automatic renewals.

Gaming Services (Xbox Game Pass, Google Play Pass)

Costs ₹300–₹700 per month, often billed automatically, and paired with in-app purchases.

Apps request unnecessary access like microphone or device storage, which can be misused if breached.

The Hidden Cybersecurity Angle

Apart from financial costs, subscriptions and apps also pose cybersecurity risks:

Excessive Data Collection

Apps collect more data than necessary (contacts, location, photos).

Unauthorized Access & Fraud

Malicious apps or APKs can steal sensitive data like OTPs, banking info, and personal messages, sometimes via screen-sharing tricks.

Data Misuse by Platforms

Some apps, especially digital lending apps, have reportedly harassed users using their personal data.

Footprint & Tracking

Every subscription increases your digital footprint, allowing apps to push more paid services subtly.

Pointers for Safer Usage

  • Audit subscriptions regularly: Cancel unused or unnecessary services.
  • Check app permissions: Avoid granting access to call logs, contacts, or microphone for apps that don't need them. OTT platforms, for example, don't need read access to your calls or contacts.
  • Use official sources: Only download apps from Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
  • Enable two-factor authentication: Adds extra security against account misuse.
  • Be mindful of free trials: Set reminders to cancel before auto-renewal if you don't intend to continue.

A Personal Take & Call to Action

I've stepped back from indulging in every subscription trap, and that's when the picture became clear: these services are designed to exploit attention, data, and habits, not just money. By understanding the technical mechanisms, UX tricks, and cyber risks, we can regain control, enjoy only the services we genuinely need, and reclaim our time, attention, and social life.

Take Action Today

Take a moment today: audit your subscriptions, revoke unnecessary permissions, and uninstall apps that collect excessive data. Reclaim your digital freedom before it quietly drains your wallet—and your life.
