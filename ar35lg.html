The Art of Letting Go: Why Control Is an Illusion

When you stop trying to control everything, you finally see how little you needed to.

Mental Resilience
November 2025
6 min read

Hey Restless Heart,

Ever felt that urge to control someone or something? That burning need to make things go exactly the way you want? Ahh, got it. We've all been there. I'm here with my views and thoughts on "The Art of Letting Go."

The Harsh Reality

I couldn't sleep. I was scrolling through news reports and surveys, and what I found honestly shook me. Our generation is drowning, and nobody's talking about the real reason why.

Someone ending their life because things didn't go as expected. Someone taking another's life because of anger or ego. Someone falling apart mentally because they couldn't control their world anymore.

Here are a few that hit me the hardest:

  • A Times of India report (2024) shared how a 19-year-old topper gave up after failing once in a competitive exam. One failure. That's all it took.
  • BBC News covered a story of a young startup founder who stabbed his own friend over a business fallout.
  • According to The Hindu, a 25-year-old tech employee collapsed due to overwork and stress. Colleagues revealed she hadn't slept for three days straight.
  • NDTV reported a teenage gamer who shot his friend after losing an online match.
  • India Today covered multiple cases of young couples ending their lives after relationship failures, all because they couldn't "control" how love turned out.

And the numbers that keep me up at night:

  • YourStory.com found that 57% of young Indians now use AI chatbots for emotional support. We're talking to machines because humans feel too unpredictable.
  • UNICEF discovered that only 41% of Indian youth (ages 15 to 24) believe it's "good" to seek mental health help.
  • The Times of India reported that 53% of Indian youth are "personality malnourished," lacking resilience, life skills, and inner balance.
  • Tamil Nadu Police data showed juvenile crime cases rising from 2,212 in 2021 to 2,607 in 2022.

Wondering Why I Mentioned All This?

Because these aren't just random tragedies. Every single one of these cases, every statistic, every life lost, they're all symptoms of one deep-rooted problem: our generation's obsession with control.

We've convinced ourselves that if we just try hard enough, plan perfectly enough, worry intensely enough, we can bend reality to our will. And when it doesn't work (because it never fully works), we break. Or worse, we break others.

Think about it for a second. Can you control air? Can you bottle up water and make it stay still forever? Can you tame fire without it eventually burning you?

These elements are stronger than us. They flow, they change, they move on their own rhythm. And yet here we are, trying to micromanage every person, every outcome, every tiny detail of our existence.

We were born innocent. Free. Open-hearted. Babies don't try to control anything; they just are. So why do we spend our entire lives trying to grip and force the world into submission instead of learning to live like that again?

I've been there too. The panic attacks wondering "what if things don't go my way?" The obsessive planning. The anxiety that sits in your chest like a stone when someone doesn't text back fast enough.

And here's what I learned, slowly, painfully, but truly:

5 Steps to Truly Let Go

1. Accept what's beyond your reach.
Not everything is meant to stay. Some things, and yes, some people, are just passing through your timeline. I lost a close friend last year. Not to death, just to life. We grew apart. I tried everything to "fix" it. Until I realized: some endings aren't failures. They're just... endings. And that's okay.

2. Detach, don't disconnect.
Caring doesn't mean clinging. Love doesn't mean possession. You can deeply care about someone and still give them space to be their own person, make their own choices, even their own mistakes. Let people breathe, including yourself.

3. Turn your energy inward.
You can't control the storm, but you can absolutely control your sail. Instead of exhausting yourself trying to change how others behave, invest that energy in the one thing you can control: your response. Your perspective. Your inner peace.

4. Forgive quickly.
Grudges give you this illusion of control. Like you're punishing someone by holding onto anger. But really? The only person chained up is you. Your peace is the prisoner. Forgiveness isn't about them deserving it. It's about you deserving freedom.

5. Surrender, not as defeat, but as trust.
Surrender is saying, "I trust the process more than my fear." It's saying, "I've done what I can, and now I'm letting life do what it does." It's not weakness. It's wisdom.

Final Note
I wrote this after another restless night, thinking about all those news reports, all those lives cut short because we couldn't let go. And I thought: what if just one person reads this and decides to loosen their grip a little? To breathe a little easier?

Surrender is not always a defeat. Sometimes, it's a step toward real victory. The victory of peace over anxiety. Of trust over fear. Of living over merely surviving.

Be smart, live smart, and let's make our environment (and ourselves) a little lighter, a little happier.
